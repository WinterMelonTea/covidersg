OVERVIEW

On November 2019, an outbreak involving a flu-like virus had transpired in Wuhan, China. Due to insufficient information, the virus was not able to be contained in time before it had spread worldwide by means of air travel through human hosts. Details of the virus were only discovered in early 2020, describing the virus as similar to the common flu, except that it was more infectious, but less lethal. By then, the virus had made its presence in many countries and was declared an epidemic by WHO.

Currently, the virus has infected over 7 million people and has been involved in the deaths of over a million.

This website was created to help Singaporeans obtain the latest information about COVID-19 from multiple sources in one handy place. This website also aims to present data in interactive, insightful and visually-appealing graphs. Please note that this project will see improvements and updates along the way and the current version may not be final.
